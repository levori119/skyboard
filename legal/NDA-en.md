## Recitals

**Whereas** the Disclosing Party has developed and holds proprietary information, know-how, a software product and an operational concept in the field of display, recording and operational-information management systems for control and supervision workstations (the **"System"**);

**Whereas** the Parties wish to hold discussions and conduct mutual evaluation for the purpose of exploring a potential business and/or technological collaboration between them (the **"Purpose"**);

**Whereas** for the Purpose alone, the Disclosing Party may disclose to the Receiving Party Confidential Information as defined below;

**Whereas** the Receiving Party agrees to receive the Confidential Information subject to the terms of this Agreement, and understands that but for its undertakings under this Agreement the Disclosing Party would not have disclosed the information to it at all;

**Now, therefore, the Parties have agreed, stipulated and declared as follows:**

---

## 1. Preamble and Schedules

1.1. The preamble to this Agreement and its schedules form an integral part hereof.

1.2. Section headings are for convenience of reading only and shall not be used to interpret this Agreement.

---

## 2. Definition of "Confidential Information"

2.1. **"Confidential Information"** — any information, in any form and medium, disclosed or made available to the Receiving Party by or on behalf of the Disclosing Party, directly or indirectly, whether before or after the date of execution of this Agreement, including without limiting the generality of the foregoing:

  2.1.1. The **concept and idea** underlying the System, including the operational need it is intended to solve and the manner of its solution;

  2.1.2. **Product requirements**, user stories, acceptance criteria, roadmap and development plans;

  2.1.3. **User-interface design** — screens, layouts, workflows, information hierarchy, color schemes, keyboard shortcuts and manner of interaction;

  2.1.4. The **architecture** — system structure, component breakdown, data flow, data model, database schema and API interface structure;

  2.1.5. **Algorithms and business logic**, including proprietary processing, computation and synchronization mechanisms;

  2.1.6. **Source code**, object code, technical documentation, development environments and build tools;

  2.1.7. The **terminology and professional language** developed for the System and its mapping to operational terms;

  2.1.8. **Demonstrations (demos)**, presentations, screenshots, video recordings, demo clips and prototypes;

  2.1.9. **Business information** — business model, pricing, existing and potential customers, partners, suppliers, marketing plans and financial data;

  2.1.10. The **very existence of the discussions** between the Parties, their content, their stage of progress and the identity of the Parties to them;

  2.1.11. Any **derivative, analysis, abstract, summary, note or document** prepared by or on behalf of the Receiving Party, to the extent it is based on or reflects the Confidential Information.

2.2. **The Confidential Information is protected under this Agreement whether disclosed in writing, orally, visually, electronically or in any other manner, and whether or not expressly marked "confidential."** Failure to mark information as confidential shall not derogate from its status as Confidential Information.

2.3. Information disclosed orally or by visual demonstration shall be deemed Confidential Information from the moment of its disclosure, and the Disclosing Party is under no obligation to confirm it in writing after the fact.

---

## 3. Exceptions to the Confidentiality Obligation

3.1. The Receiving Party's obligations under this Agreement shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party proves, **by written, verifiable evidence created contemporaneously**, meets one of the following:

  3.1.1. Was **in the public domain** at the time of its disclosure to the Receiving Party;

  3.1.2. Became **part of the public domain** after its disclosure, other than as a result of any act or omission of the Receiving Party or anyone on its behalf and not through any breach of a confidentiality obligation;

  3.1.3. Was **lawfully in the Receiving Party's possession** prior to its disclosure by the Disclosing Party, free of any confidentiality obligation;

  3.1.4. Was lawfully disclosed to the Receiving Party by a **third party** entitled to disclose it, without breach of any confidentiality obligation toward the Disclosing Party;

  3.1.5. Was **independently developed** by the Receiving Party, by employees who had no access to and were not exposed to the Confidential Information, as proven by contemporaneous, verifiable development records.

3.2. **For the avoidance of doubt:** a particular compilation or combination of items of information shall not be deemed part of the public domain merely because individual items within it are in the public domain, unless the compilation or combination itself, in all its components, is in the public domain.

3.3. **Disclosure required by law** — if the Receiving Party is required to disclose Confidential Information pursuant to a judicial order, the demand of a competent authority or a provision of law, it shall notify the Disclosing Party thereof **immediately, in writing and prior to disclosure**, to the extent permitted by law, shall enable the Disclosing Party to act to prevent or limit the disclosure, and shall cooperate with it to that end at the Disclosing Party's expense. In any event, the Receiving Party shall disclose only the minimal portion of the information required by law, and shall act to ensure that it be afforded confidential treatment.

---

## 4. Undertakings of the Receiving Party

The Receiving Party hereby undertakes:

4.1. **To keep the Confidential Information in strict confidence**, and to employ for that purpose precautions and protective measures no less than those it applies to its own confidential information of similar value, and in any event no less than a reasonable standard of care.

4.2. **To use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose**, and for no other purpose whatsoever.

4.3. **Not to disclose, deliver, transfer, distribute or display** the Confidential Information to any third party, except subject to Section 5 below.

4.4. **Not to copy, reproduce, photograph, record or document** the Confidential Information, in whole or in part, except to the extent required for the Purpose. Every copy shall bear the confidentiality markings of the original, and the provisions of this Agreement shall apply to it.

4.5. **Not to perform reverse engineering**, disassembly, decompilation, structural analysis or any other act whose purpose or effect is to reveal the manner of operation of the System, including from demonstrations, screenshots or a prototype.

4.6. **Not to record, photograph or film** meetings, demonstrations or presentations of the Disclosing Party, except with its express prior written consent.

4.7. To bring to the attention of anyone exposed to the Confidential Information on its behalf the confidentiality of the information and the undertakings under this Agreement.

4.8. **To notify the Disclosing Party immediately and in writing** of any suspected loss, loss of control, unauthorized disclosure or unauthorized use of the Confidential Information, and to cooperate fully in mitigating the harm.

---

## 5. Permitted Disclosure — "Need to Know"

5.1. The Receiving Party may disclose the Confidential Information solely to its employees, officers and professional advisors (the **"Representatives"**) who:

  5.1.1. **Need to know the information** for the Purpose alone (need-to-know);

  5.1.2. Are **bound by a confidentiality undertaking** whose terms are no less restrictive than those of this Agreement;

  5.1.3. Have been **informed** in advance of the confidentiality of the information and the restrictions applicable to it.

5.2. The Receiving Party may disclose the Confidential Information to an affiliate, a subcontractor or any third party **only with the Disclosing Party's prior written consent**.

5.3. **The Receiving Party bears full responsibility** for any act or omission of its Representatives and of anyone exposed to the Confidential Information on its behalf, as if it were its own act or omission.

5.4. At the Disclosing Party's request, the Receiving Party shall provide it with an **updated written list** of all persons exposed to the Confidential Information on its behalf.

---

## 6. Non-Use, Non-Compete and Non-Circumvention

> **Material section — the core protection of this Agreement.**

6.1. The Receiving Party undertakes **not to use the Confidential Information**, directly or indirectly, for the development, specification, design, manufacture, marketing, distribution or commissioning of any product, system, service or component **that competes with the System or is similar to it in essence**, whether by itself, through another, or in cooperation with another.

6.2. The Receiving Party undertakes **not to circumvent the Disclosing Party** and not to contact, directly or indirectly, its customers, partners, suppliers or parties introduced to it by the Disclosing Party, in any matter relating to the Purpose or the System, except with the Disclosing Party's prior written consent.

6.3. The Receiving Party undertakes **not to approach or solicit**, directly or indirectly, any person employed by or providing services to the Disclosing Party, for the term of this Agreement and 12 months thereafter. A general recruitment advertisement not specifically directed at such a person shall not be deemed a breach of this Section.

6.4. **No Residuals:** The Parties expressly agree that the Receiving Party shall have **no** right to use the Confidential Information or any part thereof on the basis of any claim of "unaided memory," "general knowledge," "acquired skill," "residuals" or any similar claim. Any such use shall be deemed a breach of this Agreement.

6.5. The Receiving Party declares that **it is aware that the Confidential Information is a trade secret** of the Disclosing Party within the meaning of the Commercial Torts Law, 5759-1999, and that the Disclosing Party employs reasonable measures to preserve its confidentiality.

---

## 7. Intellectual Property

7.1. **All intellectual property rights in the Confidential Information and in the System** — including copyrights, patents and patent applications, trade secrets, industrial designs, trademarks, moral rights and any other right, registered or unregistered — are and shall remain **the sole and exclusive property of the Disclosing Party**.

7.2. **Nothing in this Agreement, in the disclosure of the Confidential Information or in the conduct of the discussions between the Parties shall grant the Receiving Party any right, license, ownership, interest or stake** in the Confidential Information or the System, whether express, implied, by estoppel or otherwise.

7.3. **The information is provided "AS IS."** The Disclosing Party makes no representation or warranty as to the accuracy, completeness or fitness of the Confidential Information for any purpose, and shall bear no liability whatsoever for the Receiving Party's reliance on it.

7.4. **Feedback:** to the extent the Receiving Party provides the Disclosing Party with comments, suggestions or ideas for improving the System — the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to make free, unrestricted use thereof, without consideration, and the Receiving Party shall acquire no right in the System as a result.

7.5. **Developments and improvements:** any improvement, development, derivative or invention created by or on behalf of the Receiving Party using or in reliance on the Confidential Information shall be **owned by the Disclosing Party alone**, and the Receiving Party hereby assigns to it in advance all rights therein.

---

## 8. No Obligation to Contract

8.1. **Nothing in this Agreement shall obligate either Party** to enter into any agreement, to continue the contacts, to effect a transaction, or to purchase, sell or provide any service.

8.2. Each Party may **terminate the contacts at any time, for any reason and without any justification**, without any cause of action arising in favor of the other Party as a result.

8.3. The confidentiality and non-use undertakings under this Agreement **shall remain in force even after the termination of the contacts**, in accordance with Section 10.

8.4. A binding agreement between the Parties shall be formed, if at all, only in a separate instrument executed by the authorized signatories of both Parties.

---

## 9. Return and Destruction

9.1. **Upon the Disclosing Party's demand at any time, and in any event upon the termination of the contacts between the Parties or the expiry of this Agreement** — whichever is earlier — the Receiving Party shall, within **7 days**:

  9.1.1. **Return** all tangible Confidential Information in its possession and every copy thereof; or

  9.1.2. **Destroy** the Confidential Information, including irreversible deletion from every computer system, storage medium, cloud service and backup, at the Disclosing Party's election.

9.2. The Receiving Party shall provide the Disclosing Party, within a further 7 days, with a **certificate signed by a senior officer** confirming full performance of the foregoing.

9.3. **Notwithstanding the foregoing** — the Receiving Party may retain one copy of the Confidential Information in its legal department, solely to the extent required for compliance with a legal provision or binding regulation, provided that the copy is kept in full confidence, is not used in any way, and the provisions of this Agreement continue to apply to it without time limit.

9.4. Such return or destruction **shall not derogate** from the Receiving Party's undertakings under this Agreement, which shall continue to apply in full.

---

## 10. Term of the Agreement

10.1. This Agreement shall enter into force upon its execution, or upon the first disclosure of Confidential Information — **whichever is earlier** — and shall also apply to Confidential Information disclosed prior to the date of execution.

10.2. **The confidentiality and non-use obligations shall remain in force for a period of 10 (ten) years** from the date of the last disclosure of Confidential Information.

10.3. **Notwithstanding Section 10.2** — with respect to Confidential Information constituting a **trade secret** as defined by law, and with respect to **source code, algorithms and the data model**, the confidentiality and non-use obligations shall remain in force **without time limit**, for so long as the information remains a trade secret.

10.4. Sections 6, 7, 9, 10, 11 and 12 shall survive the expiry of this Agreement.

---

## 11. Breach and Remedies

11.1. The Receiving Party declares that **it is aware that a breach of this Agreement is liable to cause the Disclosing Party severe and irreparable harm**, which cannot be compensated by money alone, and that quantifying the harm would be difficult or impossible.

11.2. Accordingly, in addition to any other remedy available to the Disclosing Party under law or under this Agreement, **the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to injunctive relief, mandatory injunction and any interim or permanent remedy** — without being required to prove damage and without being required to post a bond or an indemnity undertaking.

11.3. **Liquidated damages:** without derogating from any other remedy, it is agreed that for each fundamental breach of Sections 4, 5 or 6 of this Agreement, the breaching Party shall pay the Disclosing Party **agreed and pre-determined liquidated damages in the amount of \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ ILS**, without derogating from the Disclosing Party's right to claim compensation for its actual damages to the extent they exceed this amount.

> **Note to counsel:** an amount reflecting a reasonable advance estimate of the harm should be set — an excessive amount may be struck down by the court under Section 15(a) of the Contracts (Remedies for Breach of Contract) Law.

11.4. The Receiving Party **shall indemnify the Disclosing Party** for any damage, loss, expense and payment, including attorneys' fees and legal costs, incurred by it as a result of a breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party or anyone on its behalf.

## 12. Miscellaneous

12.1. **Entire agreement:** this Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties on its subject matter, and supersedes any prior representation, offer, understanding or agreement, whether written or oral.

12.2. **Amendments:** any amendment, modification or waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be made **in writing only**, signed by both Parties.

12.3. **No waiver:** a Party's forbearance from exercising a right under this Agreement, or the grant of an extension, shall not be deemed a waiver of that or any other right, and shall not preclude it from exercising the right at any time.

12.4. **Severability:** if any provision of this Agreement is found to be void, excessive or unenforceable, its scope shall be reduced to the minimum extent necessary to render it enforceable, and the remaining provisions of the Agreement shall remain in force.

12.5. **No assignment:** the Receiving Party may not assign, transfer or novate its rights or obligations under this Agreement, in whole or in part, including by way of merger, acquisition or change of control, **without the Disclosing Party's prior written consent**. The Disclosing Party may assign its rights to a successor or to an entity that will hold the System.

12.6. **Governing law and jurisdiction:** this Agreement shall be governed solely by **the laws of the State of Israel**. Exclusive jurisdiction over any matter relating to this Agreement is vested in the **competent courts of the city of \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_**, and in them alone.

12.7. **Notices:** notices under this Agreement shall be sent to the Parties' addresses as set out in the preamble, or to the email address \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_, and shall be deemed to have reached their destination: upon personal delivery — at the time of delivery; by registered mail — within 3 business days; by email — on the following business day, provided no error message has been received.
