ה-Prompt המוצע (להעתקה):Subject: Aviation Air Traffic Control (ATC) Simulation - Formation & Flight Strip Management SystemContext:Build a web-based system for managing aircraft formations (Hebrew: "פ"מ" or "מבנה"). The system must handle the logic of splitting and merging formations across different ATC positions (Ground, Tower, Radar/Strips).Core Entities & Data Model:Formation (פ"מ): Defined by a Callsign (או"ק), e.g., "BANANA".Aircraft (מטוס): Each formation consists of $N$ aircraft, indexed 1 to $N$ (e.g., BANANA 1, BANANA 2).Partial Formation (פ"מ חלקי): A subset of a formation identified by its indices, e.g., "BANANA 1+4".Functional Logic:Dynamic Naming:Full formation = <Callsign>.Partial formation = <Callsign> <index>+<index> (e.g., "BANANA 1+2+4").If all aircraft merge at the same point, the name reverts to the full <Callsign>.Position Logic:Ground Mode: Focus is on the individual aircraft. Users can drag individual aircraft to different ground points.Other Modes (Strips/Map): Focus is on the formation.Splitting & Transfer:When transferring from Ground to Strips: If only specific aircraft are moved, create a "Partial Formation" in the destination.Partial Transfer UI: In Map/Table/Strips mode, dragging a formation to a transfer point should trigger a "Partial Transfer" option, allowing the user to select specific aircraft IDs.Merging Logic:If a partial formation is dragged onto another partial formation (of the same Callsign) at the same location, they merge.Data Preservation: Upon merging, all free-text remarks and metadata from both parts must be concatenated/merged to prevent data loss.Validation:If aircraft within a merged formation have conflicting "Squawk" (ספרור) codes, display a warning: "Not all aircraft updated" (לא כל המטוסים מעודכנים).UI Requirements:Airport Map View: Ability to see and drag individual aircraft.Strip View: Visual representation of full and partial formations.Drag & Drop: Support for moving entities between positions and merging them visually.Tech Stack: React, Tailwind CSS, and Lucide-React for icons. Focus on a clean, professional "Military/Aviation" dark-mode interface.
